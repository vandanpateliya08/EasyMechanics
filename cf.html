What Are Concurrent Forces?

Concurrent forces are forces whose lines of action all pass through a single common point. When they also lie in the same plane they are called coplanar concurrent forces.

📐

Resolution

Any force F at angle θ splits into Fx = F cosθ and Fy = F sinθ along the axes.

➕

Resultant

R = √((ΣFx)² + (ΣFy)²), direction θ = arctan(ΣFy / ΣFx).

⚖️

Equilibrium

System is in equilibrium when ΣFx = 0 and ΣFy = 0, giving R = 0.

1. What is a Force?

A force is a vector quantity with magnitude (Newtons) and direction (angle). Represented as an arrow — length = magnitude, arrowhead = direction.

2. Resolution of a Force

Any force F at angle θ from the +X axis resolves into:

Fx = F cos θ   |   Fy = F sin θ

3. Resultant of a Force System

ΣFx = F₁cosθ₁ + F₂cosθ₂ + ...   |   ΣFy = F₁sinθ₁ + F₂sinθ₂ + ...
R = √( (ΣFx)² + (ΣFy)² )   |   θ = arctan( ΣFy / ΣFx )

4. Sign Convention

  • Angles measured counter-clockwise from +X axis are positive.
  • Fx > 0 → rightward  |  Fx < 0 → leftward
  • Fy > 0 → upward  |  Fy < 0 → downward

5. Equilibrium Condition

ΣFx = 0   and   ΣFy = 0   ⟹   R = 0

6. Lami's Theorem (3 Forces)

When exactly three concurrent forces are in equilibrium:

F₁ / sin α₁ = F₂ / sin α₂ = F₃ / sin α₃

7. Parallelogram Law

Two forces at a point are replaced by a single resultant — the diagonal of the parallelogram formed with those forces as adjacent sides.

8. Triangle & Polygon Law

Forces drawn head-to-tail as sides of a polygon — the closing side (reversed) gives the resultant.

9. Applications

  • Structural analysis — forces in trusses and frames
  • Rope and pulley systems — tension calculations
  • Inclined plane problems — weight components
  • Equilibrium of particles and rigid bodies
